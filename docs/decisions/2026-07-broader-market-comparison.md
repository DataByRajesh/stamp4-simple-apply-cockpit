# Competitive comparison: Stamp4 vs the broader AI job-search tool market (2026-07-22)

## Scope

Broadened beyond ApplyWave (see `2026-07-applywave-comparison.md`) to the general AI
job-search tool market: Teal, Huntr, Jobscan, Careerflow, Rezi, Simplify Copilot,
LazyApply, LoopCV, Sonara, JobCopilot, FastApply, Jobright, scale.jobs.

## Outcome

Confirmed the same conclusion as the ApplyWave comparison, at higher confidence: nothing
in the broader market combines evidence-grounded generation + visa-threshold-aware
scoring for Ireland/Netherlands/Germany + direct ATS polling the way Stamp4 does. These
tools split into:

- **Trackers** (Teal, Huntr) - pipeline management, no visa/scoring depth.
- **ATS optimizers** (Jobscan, Careerflow, Rezi) - keyword matching against a JD, no
  sponsorship logic.
- **Semi-auto** (Simplify Copilot) - free autofill, human still submits.
- **Full auto-apply bots** (LazyApply, LoopCV, Sonara, JobCopilot, FastApply) - submit on
  the user's behalf at volume.
- **Matching/discovery** (Jobright, scale.jobs) - AI-recommended roles from large pools.

None are visa/sponsorship-focused at the depth ApplyWave or Stamp4 are; all are
general/US-market tools.

## Validation of an existing decision (not a new finding to act on)

Multiple independent sources in this market segment actively warn against the auto-apply
category Stamp4 deliberately excluded: LazyApply has ~2.1 Trustpilot rating and carries
account-ban risk; employers are documented to flag high-volume generic AI applications;
one guide's core advice is "10 tailored applications beat 100 generic ones." This
independently confirms the human-in-the-loop, no-auto-submit philosophy already chosen
for AutoTime EU Apply (and therefore not replicated in Stamp4) was the right call, not an
oversight.

## Scope boundary

This is the broadest competitive sweep run to date (spans ApplyWave-adjacent tools and
the general US market). No further competitive scanning is planned unless a specific new
product is named with a stated reason to check it. If a future session considers another
market scan "to find gaps," check this doc and the ApplyWave one first - two independent
passes have already confirmed the same conclusion, and a third is unlikely to change it.
